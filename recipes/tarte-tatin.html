Tarte Tatin recipe

Description

Tarte Tatin is a pastry in which the fruit (usually apples)
is caramelized in butter and sugar before the tart is baked.
Named after the Tatin sisters who invented it and served it in their hotel as its signature dish, it originated in France
but has spread to other countries over time.

A popular myth tells that the dessert was accidentally created at the Hôtel Tatin in central France in the 1880s.

The hotel was run by two sisters, Stéphanie and Caroline Tatin.
There are a few myths about the tart's origin, the most common is that Stéphanie Tatin, who did most of the cooking, was tired one day and
tried to make a traditional apple pie, but she left the apples cooking in butter and sugar for too long.
Smelling the burning, she tried to save the dish by putting the pastry base on top of the pan of apples
and quickly finishing the cooking by putting the whole pan in the oven.

After serving the upside-down tart, she was surprised to find how much the hotel guests liked the dessert.
In a different version of the myth, Stéphanie baked a caramelized apple tart upside-down by mistake

Ingredients

Flaky Pastry

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/8 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 110g unsalted butter, cold, cut into cubes
  • 3 tablespoons ice water

Apple Filling

  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 140g unsalted butter, cut into thin slices
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 kg apples peeled and halved
  • Crème fraîche, whipped cream, or ice cream, for serving
